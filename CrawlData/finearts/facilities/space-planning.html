SPACE PLANNING
2018-2019

Concordia is undertaking a space planning exercise looking ten years into the future. Over the next six months, our Faculty of Fine Arts is actively connecting with faculty, staff and students, along with colleagues from Facilities Management, to collaboratively identify needs and desires for the future.

Mandate

Presently, the university as a whole is evaluating different alternatives to address future space needs in the next ten years, related to the projection of enrolment growth. The expansion and transformation of facilities is planned according to the Strategic Directions of the university. For the elaboration of a Master space plan for Concordia University for 2018-2028, a thorough space analysis of all the Faculties, including Fine Arts, is required.

Phase 1 (October 2018 to January 2019)

The first phase mainly encompasses consultation with internal and external constituents; research, documentation and analysis of data on existing space; comprehensive overview of research and teaching activities within FoFA; presentation of teaching and research trends in the fields of art, arts-based scholarship, performance, and design in higher education. The objective is to obtain a portrait of the space utilisation by FoFA and to start to articulate the vision of the facilities required in the larger context of the development of university facilities.

  • Analysis of the existing space inventory for FoFA, related to space categories, as per the Ministry of Education and Higher Education space norm.
  • Study of project precedents for significant art, design and architecture schools
  • Comprehensive tour of existing facilities to record Slived experience of usage in relation to data-driven analysis; documentation of unexpected Sspace hacks and improvisatory interstitial activities
  • Organisation of the following consultation activities, with compilation and analysis of data, production of meeting notes, in collaboration with key people from FoFA and Facilities Management:
    • Follow-up survey on appreciation of facilities by the FoFA students, faculty, and staff (the Faculty has performed an initial survey in 2018) with the collaboration with the Supervisor of Academic and Research Facilities in the Office of the Dean and University Communications Services.
    •  Multiple group discussions and departmental visits with FoFA students, faculty and staff and other guests on space planning topics related to the    university strategic directions.

Phase 2 (January 2019 to April 2019)

The second phase includes the specification of the space needs and the production of a detailed functional space program.

  •  Production of a detailed space list
  •  Space scenario
    • Description of key spaces (sense of space)
    • Illustrated description of space types
    • Schematic illustration of the space organisation and design intentions
    • Speculative synergy diagrams for internal and external collaboration
      between program areas  

Process

Consultation, compilation and analysis of activities undertaken by the Faculty of Fine Arts will take place in three formats:

IDEALABS

Three open Idealabs were conducted in October and November that cut across different thematics: Experimental Pedagogy, Research Creation, and STEAM (Science, Technology, Engineering, Arts, and Math). Over two dozen presenters and over 100 participants assembled in different venues across the Fine Arts to hear about current activity in the Faculty. These brief expositions seeded subsequent group discussions about future space configurations rooted in needs, desires, and realities. Documentation of each Idealab will soon be posted on this website.

Watch IdeaLabs videos

FIELDWORK

In early 2019 a robust "in the field" data gathering exercise and public dissemination project led by a team of paid student "Rovers" will take place. Led by MFA student Breanna Shanahan with input from Faculty mentors and architects Ana Maria Llanos of Diamond Schmitt Architects and Dominique Dumont from Concordia Facilities Management, the Fieldworkers will perform three main tasks (1) comb the Faculty to gather data about space needs, (2) focus on specific questions and spatial problematics arising from consultations to develop case study analyses, and (3) respond to and communicate publicly "findings" from the field using a variety of fine arts methodologies such as photo essays, zines, theatrical re-enactments, speculative fiction, documentary practices, audio or visual reflections, reflection-in-action reports, etc. Results will be presented to the community through event-based Field Reports with documentation posted on this website.
 
Our twelve Rovers are now out working in the field, gathering data, images, sounds and interviews for their projects. They have each been tasked with two projects to work on, which they will present in EV 2.776 on the following dates from noon - 4 p.m.:

  • First presentation: Friday, February 15
  • Second presentation: Friday, March 15

MOONSHOT

The final (and fun!) culmination of Faculty activity focused on collective imaginings will be a speculative design charrette in the spring hosted by a guest futurist. Moonshot will create a forum for us to imagine a Sci-Fi Faculty of the Future that pushes us to the limit of our ability to predict the unknown while still staying true to our ideals of critical practice, disciplinary depth, cross-medial exchange, social inclusion, and community engagement. What openings and closings will the far future bring? How will space, time, things, texts, and bodies be accommodated? What will knowledge look and feel like? What kind of citizens do we want to be? What kinds of architectures will support our vision?

CONSULTATION

The majority of the space planning work Concordia is undertaking with Diamond Schmitt and Associates consists of many forms of one-on-one and group consultations: tours, visits, workshops, charrettes and interviews with staff, faculty and students from across our nine departments and units. Events involving over 120 selected members of the Faculty of Fine Arts will focus on the future of Fine Arts physical spaces taking into consideration the strategic directions of the University.
