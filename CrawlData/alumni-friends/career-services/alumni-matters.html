Living and working sustainably - Roundtables II

Connect with Lukas Brochard on LinkedIn.

Carbonfootprint.com has a calculator that lets you estimate your carbon footprint.

How to reduce your carbon footprint basic principles:

  • Think before you buy - do I need this?
  • Buy local  - is it made here?
  • Buy in season - is this meant to be grown at this time of year?
  • Use active or public transport - can I get there without my car?
  • When making a large purchase (car, refrigerator, stove, cell phone) think if you really need to replace and if so do a little research about the best options (plug in hybrid? Full electric? Energystar? Fairphone?).
  • When investing your savings/money look at the green" options: CoPower,  Green bonds or ESG conscious portfolios like Ishares MSCI social 400.
  • Avoid plastic as much as possible and reduce your meat consumption, especially read meat!
  • Look for green certifications" (they are not a perfect solution but they bring us closer to better practices, especially in farming)
  • Try to work from home at least 1 day a week.

For further reading:

Silent Spring, Cradle to Cradle, The ecology of commerce, This changes everything

Places to look for jobs in the field of sustainability: acre.com, engages.ca, novae.ca
