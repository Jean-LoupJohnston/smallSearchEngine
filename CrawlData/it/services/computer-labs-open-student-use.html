Computer & media labs - open use

Concordia offers a wide range of computing resources, electronic, and media-related equipment to the academic community.The labs on this page are open for use by all Concordia students and do not require a reservation or access code.
If you would like to reserve a lab, please visit our teaching use page. The labs on that page may be reserved for academic or work-related purposes.

Please contact your particular department for more information about their departmental labs.
 

Related policy: Policy on computing facilities VPS-30

Who can use it?

Faculty, staff, students, & alumni

How much does it cost?

There is no cost associated with this service.

How to get it

Active students, employees, and faculty have access to the open use labs with their Concordia netname without reservation.

Service availability

For specific operating hours, select a lab from the documentation section below.

FAQ

What are the lab regulations?

1. Food and drink are strictly prohibited.
2. Phone calls should not be answered in the lab.
3. Users must treat the eqipment with respect, and be conscious of the students around them.

How do I log into the computer?

To access the computer, you must log in using the same netname and password as the MyConcordia Portal.

Can I install software on a lab computer?

Only licensed software can be installed on University computers.  

What is the standard software package installed on PC lab and classroom computers?

While other software may be present, there is a standard software package installed on PC workstations in classrooms, open-use labs, and teaching labs.

This package includes:

  • Microsoft Office 2010 (including Word, PowerPoint, Excel, Access)
  • SPSS, Mathematica, Maple
  • 7zip
  • FastStone Image Viewer
  • FireFox, Internet Explorer, Chrome
  • Sophos Anti-Virus
  • iTunes, Quicktime
  • Adobe Acrobat Reader
  • Flash plugin, Java plugin, Microsoft Silverlight plugin
  • VLC Player

How do I book a lab?

The 'open use' labs cannot be reserved. For a list of labs that can be reserved, take a look through the labs available for teaching use.

Can I access a Library workstation if Iâ¬"m not a member of the Concordia community?

You may be eligible to access the Library workstations if you meet certain conditions. Visit the Guest Accounts page for more information.
